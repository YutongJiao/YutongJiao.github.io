Anomaly Detection of Water Pipeline with Video Data Analysis

Challenges and Motivations

1. Inspection by a robot travelling along the pipelines creates many hours of videos. The inspectors need to check each video frame manually to identify anomalies.
2. An automated office-based survey of varied structural deficiencies remains as a challenge for CCTV-based water pipeline inspection to release the inspectors’ workload and ensure the consistent quality of the survey.
3. To further empower the CCTV inspection process, advanced data analytic capabilities are needed for inspection interpretation and system operation.

Method

This research proposes an automated key-frame identification process to facilitate the CCTV data analysis for water pipeline inspection. The proposed framework achieves great performance with superiority in representation learning and extraction for video analysis. The workload of the office-based survey is reduced, which will reduce the possibility of errors occurred during the inspection due to the inspectors’ fatigue.

Experiment and Results

The raw dataset is randomly split into five equal sized subsamples. Among the five subsamples, one subsample is retained as the testing data of the model, and the remaining four subsamples are used as training data. The cross-validation process is then repeated five times, where five results can be collected and then be calculated to produce an average estimation.
